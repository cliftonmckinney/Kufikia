Questions?

At Kufikia our goal is simple: we want to help people learn new technologies so that they can advance both personally and technologically. We think the best way to do this is via mentor-driven virtual classes, and we're building a platform that makes this possible.

Our classes are for designed for experienced programmers only. If you have a CS degree, or if you've written code as a part of your job, you qualify. We'll also occasionally accept students with strong analytical backgrounds (statistics, finance, etc.) and novice students from underrepresented minorities.

1. Who is this for?
This program is generally for programmers who are already working with a particular technology, but want to learn something new. People with CS or MIS degrees, or who have done some software development as a part of their careers are encouraged to apply. If you're a novice with a lot of math skills and/or extensive experience with financial software there's a chance you can make it through, though you can expect the time commitment to increase significantly.
2. I haven't done programming before. Can I do this?
The short answer is probably not. If you’re starting from scratch, you may want to learn html/css/javascript first. You can do so at a number of sites, including Codecademy, Treehouse, and Udacity. There are also extensive open university courses available to teach you the basics. Check out iTunes U for more.
3. Does the program have scheduled meetings or training?
No. The program is self-serve and online so that you can do the work when you have the availability. We’ve gathered the best materials available and have developed a program that will take you from zero to hero in nine weeks. Deliverables will be required by a certain date, and you’ll have a mentor available to you should you have questions.
4. I’m a company and I’d like to sponsor a cohort/suggest a technology. What do I do?
Fill out this form or email us at hi@kufikia.com
5. Is there any obligation?
No. Our goal is to help new developers gain relevant skills, to help developers using stale technologies get up to date, and to generally raise the tech tide everywhere. Of course we hope that some of our sponsor companies find great new team members out of the whole thing, but we (and they) understand that it doesn’t always happen.

More Questions?

Kufikia is in development, so we don't have all the answers quite yet, but if you'd like to ask a specific question please do so. We'll do our best to answer in a timely fashion.
