Questions?

At Kufikia our goal is simple: we want to help people learn new technologies so that they can advance both personally and technologically. We think the best way to do this is via mentor-driven virtual classes, and we're building a platform that makes this possible.

Program sponsors provide mentors (five per cohort at one to two hours per week) and a cash incentive for program graduates. In return, companies will help us make the world a better place, and will have the chance to hire job-seeking program graduates.

1. What do company sponsors get out of this?
First and foremost, you'll have the opportunity to help people grow, both personally and professionally. Additionally, some of our students will be looking for their first or their next job in the tech world. You'll have the unique opportunity to pre-screen these students for both ability and cultural fit, and then hire the best of them. In that way, our program works a lot like an internship, but at a fraction of the cost.
2. How do you match students and companies?
We do a number of things to ensure that companies and students are well-matched. First, we evaluate the students and assess their potential to complete the course. Then, we group students according to geography. Finally, we ensure that each cohort has a consistent mix of mid-career vs. entry-level and job seeking vs. personal growth seeking students.
3. What is the mentor commitment?
We expect mentors to make themselves available for up to two hours per week. Most of this time will be spent answering questions and helping students overcome challenges. Some of the mentor time will also be spent assessing the quality of student deliverables.
4. Will I be able to hire these students when they graduate?
It depends. Some students will take these classes to advance their knowledge, but not necessarily in order to find a new job. Many will entertain new options, however.
5. Why should companies have to pay?
A lot of developers attempt to learn a new technology, but then quit. They quit because they get frustrated and give up, or because life gets in the way. We provide mentors so that the frustration is minimized, and we provide both money and a small team so that the motivation to succeed trumps the little things that tend to get in the way. Our program is like a virtual internship in many ways, except it costs a lot less, and requires a lot less effort from current staff.

More Questions?

Kufikia is in development, so we don't have all the answers quite yet, but if you'd like to ask a specific question please do so. We'll do our best to answer in a timely fashion.
