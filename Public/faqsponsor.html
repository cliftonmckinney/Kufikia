Questions about sponsorship?

We want to help people learn new technologies so that they can advance both personally and technologically. We're building a platform that makes this possible.

Program sponsors provide mentors and a cash incentive for program graduates. In return, companies will help us make the world a better place, and will have the chance to hire job-seeking program graduates.

1. What do company sponsors get out of this?
First and foremost, you'll have the opportunity to help people grow, both personally and professionally. Additionally, some of our students will be looking for their next job in the tech world. You'll have the unique opportunity to pre-screen these students for both ability and cultural fit, and then hire the best of them. In that way, our program works a lot like an internship, but at a fraction of the cost and overhead.
2. How do you match students and companies?
We do a number of things to ensure that companies and students are well-matched. First, we evaluate the students and assess their potential to complete the course. Only experienced developers or those with a tremendous amount of potential are accepted. Then, we group students according to geography. Companies are generally paired with students from their region, unless they specifically say that geography isn't important.
3. What is the mentor commitment?
We expect mentors to make themselves available for up to one hour per week. The mentor commitment is very structured. Students will have the opportunity to interact with designated mentors at a specific time each week. During this time students will have the opportunity to ask questions and seek guidance. Mentors will also get the chance to get to know the students during this time and assess whether they'd potentially be a good fit for the sponsor company.
4. Will I be able to hire these students when they graduate?
It depends. Some students will take these classes to advance their knowledge, but not necessarily in order to find a new job. Many will entertain new options, however.
